C-Section Recovery Guide

Your guide to recovery, care, and support after a cesarean delivery

Preparing for Recovery

Learn what to expect and how to care for yourself and your baby after a C-section.

Treat Your C-Section Wound with Care

After a C-section, rest and proper care are essential for recovery. Follow these tips:

  • Get enough rest: Avoid lifting heavy objects and rest whenever your baby sleeps.
  • Seek pain relief: Take medications like ibuprofen or acetaminophen as recommended by your doctor.

Look for Signs of Infection

Check your wound regularly and contact your healthcare provider if you notice:

  • Redness, swelling, or fluid leakage from the wound.
  • Fever or changes in the wound's color.

Breastfeeding Positions

Try these positions for comfort and support:

  • Football hold: Use a pillow along your side and hold your baby at your side, facing your breast.
  • Side-lying hold: Lie on your side with your baby facing your breast for feeding.

Postpartum Checkups

Stay connected with your healthcare provider for ongoing support. Key topics to discuss:

  • Your emotional well-being and mood.
