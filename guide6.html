9 Things to Do Before Going Into Labor

Welcome, Moms-to-be!

You’ve been counting down to your baby’s arrival for months. The time is near! Knowing what to expect and being prepared can make your delivery experience smoother.

Dr. Jessica W. Kiley, obstetrician and gynecologist, explains how to prepare.

1. Have Your Gear Ready

Pack your hospital bag with essentials like clothes, toiletries, and baby items. Don’t forget the car seat!

2. Stay Positive

Trust your team, stay flexible, and focus on meeting your baby!

3. Recognize False Alarms

Braxton Hicks contractions are irregular and go away with rest or hydration.

4. Know When to Go to the Hospital

During early labor, rest, hydrate, and eat lightly. Go to the hospital when labor becomes active.

5. Recognize Red Flags

Heavy bleeding, severe pain, or decreased baby movement are warning signs. Contact your doctor immediately.

6. Understand Labor Induction

Labor may be induced for medical reasons. Discuss options with your doctor.

7. Plan for Pain Relief

Epidurals and other pain relief methods are available. Talk to your care team about your preferences.

8. Have a Birth Plan

Create a flexible birth plan covering your preferences, but be prepared for adjustments.

9. Meet Your Medical Team

Your team includes doctors, nurses, and consultants who are there to guide you every step of the way.
