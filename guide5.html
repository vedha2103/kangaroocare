The Second Stage of Childbirth

Overview

Once labor is complete, you've entered the second stage of childbirth: it's time to push and deliver your baby. You're almost at the finish line!

With cervical dilation complete, this stage focuses on helping your baby through the birth canal by pushing.

How Long Does It Take?

Delivery generally takes 30 minutes to an hour but can last up to three hours for first-time mothers. For subsequent births, it often happens more quickly.

What Will You Feel?

During this stage, your contractions are more regular, lasting 60 to 90 seconds and spaced two to five minutes apart. You may feel:

  • Pain with contractions
  • An overwhelming urge to push
  • Rectal pressure
  • A tingling or burning sensation as the baby crowns
  • A slippery, wet feeling as the baby emerges

How to Push

Follow these tips to push effectively:

  • Push as if you're having a bowel movement and relax your body.
  • Tuck your chin to your chest to focus your pushes.
  • Push efficiently to speed up the process.
  • Change positions if necessary to help move the baby down.
  • Rest between contractions to conserve energy.
  • Follow your practitioner’s instructions, especially if they ask you to stop pushing temporarily.

What Happens After Delivery?

Once the baby’s head and body are out, your practitioner will:

  • Suction mucus from the baby’s nose and mouth.
  • Clamp and cut the umbilical cord.
  • Hand your baby to you for skin-to-skin contact.

The nurses will evaluate your baby, ensure they are stable, and perform routine procedures such as weighing and applying protective ointments.
